Connect Four

What is the app?__ A replica of the classic kids' game, the goal is to connect four of your colored "disks" in a line (horizontal, vertical, or diagonal) before your opponent does.

Why make the app?__ An exit assignment designed to test students on three things:

  • Their ability to interact with and manipulate the DOM using JavaScript
  • Their comprehension and execution of more advanced concepts (functions, conditionals, etc.)
  • Their ability to solve a problem -- "how can we mimic gravity and Connect Four's verticality?" -- using anticipatory decomposition.

Mad Libs

What is the app?__ A replica of the classic word game, the goal is to produce a new, more-amusing account of the discovery of the first "computer bug," by replacing key words (nouns, verbs, etc.) with user-supplied ones.

DnD Aid

What is the app?__ A full-stack app, using Vue.js Ruby on Rails, HTML and CSS, which facilitates user-associated character creation.

Why make the app?__ For my capstone project at Actualize bootcamp, I wanted to create a well-rounded app that explored

  • user and character database CRUD actions,
  • user authentication,
  • Frontend design emphasizing UX/UI best practices, and
  • fetching data from external APIs for local database population.

Simple JS Encryptor-Decryptor

What is the app?__ A basic encryption and decryption app that uses an Atbash cipher to encrypt and decrypt text-based messages.

Why make the app?__ An exit assignment I gave to JavaScript students, which was designed to test their understanding and implementation of both JS objects and algorithms. Their goal, at minimum, was to perform encryption on a single word.

Note: Since assigning it, I have expanded the app to handle nearly all punctuation, numbers, and be able to decrypt the result back to the user's original input.__
